# What Tolaria does not model

The simulator is an honestly scoped subset of Magic. Every item below is a known divergence, with the direction it pushes the numbers. This file is generated by `tolaria limits --markdown`.


## Rules

- **engine.layers.dependencies** (CR 613.8): static abilities read base characteristics; layer dependency ordering is not resolved. _rare wrong power/toughness or type when one continuous effect depends on another_
- **engine.ward.untargetable** (CR 702.113): ward is modeled as untargetable by opponents rather than as an additional cost or tax. _warded permanents are harder to interact with than on paper; the ward cost is never paid_
- **engine.triggers.whitelist** (CR 603): only a fixed set of ~13 trigger conditions fire; others are inert. _cards keyed on unlisted events (storm count, prowess-likes, cast-your-Nth) underperform_
- **engine.replacements.minimal** (CR 614): only doubling, damage prevention, and dies-to-exile replacement effects exist. _other replacement effects are ignored, distorting engines built on them_
- **engine.protection.color-only** (CR 702.16): protection is modeled from colors only, not from qualities like artifacts or a card name. _non-color protection has no effect in combat or targeting_
- **engine.combat.menace-solo-block** (CR 509.1c): a single legal blocker against a menace creature is silently dropped rather than declared illegal. _menace is slightly stronger than it should be in lone-blocker spots_
- **engine.caps.forced-draw**: games hitting the turn cap (60 duel, 100 commander) or the decision cap are scored as draws. _grindy control mirrors and combo loops are undercounted as draws rather than resolved_
- **engine.game-one-only** (CR 100.4): every match is game one; sideboards are parsed but never brought in. _post-board configurations, hate cards, and transformational plans are absent_

## Cards

- **cards.tier.proxy**: Proxy-tier cards keep body, cost, and keywords but their rules text is not modeled. _abilities on a Proxy card simply do not happen; a deck heavy in them plays weaker than it reads_
- **cards.tier.partial**: Partial-tier cards model the main effect and drop listed rider clauses (disclosed per card). _the dropped rider never fires; usually minor, occasionally the point of the card_
- **cards.tier.unplayable**: Unplayable-tier cards cannot be cast or resolved and sit dead in the deck. _a slot that does nothing, so decks needing that card underperform_
- **cards.compiler-version**: coverage reflects one compiler version; a newer version can regrade cards. _numbers are only comparable within a compiler version, stamped on every result_

## Pilot

- **pilot.no-search**: every decision is a one-ply scoring pass with no lookahead, sequencing plan, or combo execution. _decks that reward planned lines (combo, control) win less than a skilled pilot would take_
- **pilot.sorcery-speed**: the pilot casts on its own main phases and does not hold instants for the opponent's turn. _reactive decks that want to hold up removal or counters play too proactively_
- **pilot.fidelity-heuristic**: the low-fidelity flag is a creature count (under 10), not real archetype analysis. _creature-based combo is not flagged; a fair creature-light deck is flagged the same as degenerate combo_

## Statistics

- **stats.draws-half-win**: draws count as half a win for both sides in every win rate and confidence interval. _matchups that draw often are pulled toward 50% rather than reported as unresolved_
- **stats.panic-sample-shrink**: a game that panics the engine is dropped from the sample (its count is reported separately). _a deterministically panicking card silently shrinks the sample instead of failing loudly_

## Meta

- **meta.consensus-list**: each opposing archetype is one consensus list built from its real decklists, min 3 lists. _within-archetype list variance is collapsed, so a matchup is one point estimate, not a spread_
- **meta.share-by-frequency**: meta share weights archetypes by how often they appear, not by how they placed. _a popular but losing deck weighs as much as its play rate, not its win rate_
